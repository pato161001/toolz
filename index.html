What it's good at
  • Hard data — BPM, key, time signature, energy, danceability, valence from real audio analysis
  • Two ways in — Spotify URL (uses ReccoBeats data) or a local file (analysed in your browser)
  • Local mode = real timestamps — boundaries detected from energy changes, not guessed
  • Genre, era, scene — described with proper terminology
  • Production notes — mix, fidelity, vocal style, instrumentation
  • Form-ready — every field is one click to copy
Where you'll need to verify
  • Spotify timestamps are estimates — Gemini guesses based on length and conventional structure. Play the track and edit.
  • Local timestamps are real but imperfect — major shifts caught, subtle ones may not be.
  • Section labels are AI-assigned — even with real timestamps, naming a chunk "verse" vs "chorus" is a best guess.
  • Chord progressions are educated guesses — for famous songs often correct; otherwise a starting point.
  • Obscure or very recent tracks — descriptive analysis becomes generic. Hard data (BPM, key) still accurate.
Suggested workflow
  • Spotify mode: paste link → analyse → open the track in Spotify → edit timeline cells while listening
  • Local mode: drop your file → optionally name it → analyse. Real BPM and section boundaries come from the audio.
  • Read each field, fix anything off
  • Click any timeline cell to edit, click any tag to remove, use + add row for missing sections
  • Copy fields one-by-one or use copy all at the end
